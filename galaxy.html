The Milky Way consists of Sun, eight planets, their moons, dwarf planets, asteroids, comets, and other celestial bodies held together by gravity. At its core is the Sun, a huge ball of helium and hydrogen that produces heat and light. Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune are gas giants or ice giants, while Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars are rocky inner planets. The Kuiper Belt and the far-off Oort Cloud, which is home to comets and icy bodies, are located beyond Neptune, whereas the asteroid belt is situated between Mars and Jupiter. Because of Earth's special location, life can flourish there. Each planet has unique characteristics, such as Saturn's rings, Jupiter's Great Red Spot, and Mars' possible previous water. Numerous things about the universe can be learned from the dynamic and complex solar system. The graphic talks about all the planets our solar system.
